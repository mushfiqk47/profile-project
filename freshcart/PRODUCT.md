# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Urban Dhaka residents (Gulshan, Banani, Dhanmondi, Lalmatia, Uttara) who value premium, organic, and locally-sourced groceries. They are health-conscious professionals, busy parents, and home cooks who want farm-fresh produce delivered fast — not the cheapest option, but the best quality. They shop on mobile and desktop, expect 30-minute express delivery, and trust a brand that curates rather than dumps a catalog.

## Product Purpose

FreshCart is a premium grocery delivery platform that curates organic, local, and high-quality produce for Dhaka's urban neighborhoods. It differentiates through editorial presentation, curated selections (not endless aisles), and a seamless delivery experience with 30-minute express, same-day, and scheduled slots. Success is a customer who feels they're shopping at a neighborhood market, not scrolling a warehouse.

## Brand Personality

Premium, Fresh, Trustworthy. The voice is confident without being loud — a knowledgeable neighborhood curator who hand-picks the best Rajshahi mangoes and Padma Hilsha. Warm but not folksy. Editorial but not pretentious. The brand says "we inspected this for you" rather than "buy now."

## Anti-references

- **Instacart / Chaldal**: Generic grocery app aesthetic — utilitarian grids, dense product lists, no personality, warehouse-catalog feel. FreshCart is the opposite: curated, editorial, every product earns its place.
- **Generic AI e-commerce sites**: Cream/sand backgrounds, identical card grids, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows on every section, gradient text, glassmorphism as default. The saturated 2025-2026 AI look that screams "template."
- **Bangladeshi grocery competitors**: Discount-driven, cluttered, promotional-banner-heavy interfaces that prioritize volume over curation.

## Design Principles

1. **Curate, don't dump.** Every product, every section, every pixel earns its place. Fewer items presented beautifully beat endless scrolling through mediocrity.
2. **Show the provenance.** Farmers, regions, and sourcing stories matter. The brand is built on trust through transparency — where food comes from is as important as what it is.
3. **Editorial confidence.** Typography carries the brand. Asymmetric layouts, bold display type, and generous whitespace signal premium without saying "premium."
4. **Speed is a feature, not a footnote.** 30-minute delivery isn't buried in fine print — it's a design hero. Motion and interaction should feel as fast as the service.
5. **Warmth through craft, not decoration.** The warm palette and organic shapes come from the brand's connection to farms and food, not from adding decorative elements.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance target
- All interactive elements have visible focus states
- Color contrast verified for body text (4.5:1 minimum)
- Alt text on all product and hero images
- Mobile-first responsive design (375px minimum)
- Reduced motion support via prefers-reduced-motion
- BDT currency formatting with proper locale
